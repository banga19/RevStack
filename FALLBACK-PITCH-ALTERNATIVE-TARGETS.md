# Fallback Plan: Alternative B2B Targets & Pitch Playbook

**Use this if Ultimo Trading says no, or to build your pipeline beyond the anchor client.**

---

## Framework: How to Identify Your Ideal Fallback Client

Not every trading company is a good fit. You need a specific profile.

### Ideal Client Profile (ICP)

| Criteria | Must-Have | Nice-to-Have |
|---|---|---|
| Business type | B2B wholesale, distribution, import/export, or logistics | Already has a website |
| Lead volume | Gets 10+ inquiries/month (website, phone, WhatsApp, social) | Has a WhatsApp Business account |
| Pain point | Manual follow-up, slow response, no CRM, leads going cold | Has tried automation before (even unsuccessfully) |
| Decision maker | Founder, CEO, or Sales Director — accessible | Tech-savvy or open to new tools |
| Budget | Can afford KES 50,000-150,000/month retainer | Has a marketing budget |
| Location | Kenya (or East Africa for same timezone) | Nairobi/Mombasa (easier to meet if needed) |
| Revenue | KES 5M+/year (can justify the investment) | Growing year-over-year |

## The 5-Step Fallback Sequence (When They Say No)

When a prospect says no, use this systematic follow-up sequence. Most deals close on follow-up 3-5, not the first contact.

### Sequence

| Touch | Timing | Channel | Message |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Initial outreach** | Day 0 | WhatsApp/Email | The pitch above |
| **Follow-up 1** | Day 4 | Email | "Quick follow-up on my note below. Here's a 2-minute video showing exactly what the system looks like: [Loom link]" |
| **Follow-up 2** | Day 8 | LinkedIn + WhatsApp | "Saw [Company] is growing — congrats! If lead volume is picking up, my offer for a free Pipeline Audit still stands. No obligation. Just data." |
| **Follow-up 3** | Day 15 | Email | "Here's a case study from another [sector] company that automated their lead flow: [link]. They went from 10 leads/month to 45 in 6 weeks. Worth 15 minutes?" |
| **Follow-up 4** | Day 30 | WhatsApp | "Last check-in! If timing isn't right, no worries. I'll touch base in a quarter. In the meantime, feel free to reach out if lead management becomes a priority." |
| **Re-engage** | Month 3 | Email | "Quarterly check-in! Any changes in your lead volume? If things have picked up, the offer for a free audit is still available." |

**Rule:** No response after 5 touches + 1 quarterly check-in? Remove from active list. They're not ready. Move on.

## Objection Handling Scripts

### Objection 1: "We're too small / We don't get enough leads"

> "That's actually the best time to set this up. Right now, every lead 
> matters — losing even one is painful. Automation means you can respond 
> to every single inquiry instantly, 24/7, without hiring anyone. It 
> makes a small team operate like a large one."

### Objection 2: "This sounds expensive"

> "The setup is a one-time investment, and the monthly retainer is less 
> than what you'd pay a junior salesperson. In fact, if the system doesn't 
> pay for itself within 2 months through improved conversion, I'll 
> refund your setup fee. I only win if you win."

### Objection 3: "Our team won't use it"

> "I design these systems to reduce your team's workload, not add to it. 
> The chatbot handles the repetitive stuff — answering basic questions, 
> collecting info, sending follow-ups. Your team only gets involved for 
> high-value conversations. Most teams love it within a week."

### Objection 4: "We tried automation before and it didn't work"

> "A lot of automation fails because it's either too complex or not 
> tailored to how your business actually works. My approach is different: 
> I start with a free audit to understand your exact flow, then build 
> only what solves your specific problems. No bloat, no unnecessary 
> features. And I'm here to maintain and optimize it — it's not a 
> set-it-and-forget-it project."

### Objection 5: "We need to think about it / Talk to the team"

> "Totally fair. Let me send you a one-page summary of what we discussed, 
> including the ROI numbers. Review it with your team, and I'll follow up 
> in a few days. In the meantime, here's a link to book a follow-up call 
> if you want to dive deeper: [QMe link]."

## Daily Outreach Cadence (Once You're Pitching Multiple Targets)

If Ultimo Trading says no, you pivot immediately to pitching multiple targets simultaneously. Here's your daily routine:

| Time | Activity | Target Volume |
|---|---|---|
| **8:00-8:30** | Review previous day's responses, reply to any that came in overnight | ~10 min review |
| **8:30-10:00** | LinkedIn: Find 10 new decision-makers at target companies, send connection requests with note | 10 connections |
| **10:00-11:00** | Write and send 5 personalized cold emails | 5 emails |
| **11:00-12:00** | WhatsApp outreach to 5-8 target companies | 5-8 messages |
| **12:00-1:00** | Follow-up on previous touches (Day 4, 8, 15) | 5-10 follow-ups |
| **Afternoon** | Discovery calls with any prospects who responded | As booked via QMe |

**Weekly target:** 35 new contacts + 35 follow-ups = 70 touches/week  
**Expected conversion:** 1-3% → 1-2 discovery calls/week → 1 client every 2-3 weeks

**By the numbers:**
- 70 touches/week × 4 weeks = 280 prospects contacted
- 2% conversion to discovery call = 5-6 calls
- 50% conversion to client = 2-3 new clients/month

# 18 Essential Documents Every Startup Should Have

1. **Founder Agreement** — defines roles, responsibilities, equity split, and decision-making authority among co-founders
2. **Incorporation Documents** — certificate of incorporation, articles of association, and registration with relevant authorities
3. **Co-founder Exit Clause** — outlines what happens if a founder leaves: vesting schedule, buyout terms, and IP ownership
4. **Shareholders’ Agreement** — governs share transfers, dividend policy, voting rights, and dispute resolution
5. **Cap Table** — tracks equity ownership across founders, investors, and option pool
6. **ESOP Agreement** — employee stock ownership plan terms, vesting schedule, and exercise rights
7. **NDA (Non-Disclosure Agreement)** — protects confidential information shared with employees, partners, and investors
8. **IP Assignment Agreement** — ensures all work product and IP created by team members belongs to the company
9. **Trademark / IP Documents** — registration certificates, trademark licenses, and IP protection records
10. **Employee Contracts** — employment agreements with salary, benefits, notice period, and confidentiality terms
11. **Offer Letters** — formal job offers outlining role, compensation, start date, and contingencies
12. **HR Policies** — code of conduct, leave policy, expense policy, and employee handbook
13. **Terms of Service** — governs the relationship between the platform and its users/customers
14. **Privacy Policy** — describes how user data is collected, stored, processed, and shared
15. **Legal Compliance Docs** — industry-specific licenses, tax registrations, and regulatory filings
16. **Pitch Deck** — investor presentation covering problem, solution, traction, team, and financial ask
17. **Financial Model** — revenue projections, expense budget, cash flow forecast, and unit economics
18. **Term Sheet (if funded)** — summarizes investment terms: valuation, equity stake, liquidation preference, and governance rights

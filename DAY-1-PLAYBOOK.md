## PART 3: The Discovery Call — Exact Script

### Pre-Call Prep

Before the call starts:
- [ ] Have your screen ready to share (showing their website and social pages)
- [ ] Have your notes on the gaps you found ready
- [ ] Have a blank Google Doc or Miro board ready to map their flow together
- [ ] **Record the call** (ask permission) for later reference

### Call Structure (30 Minutes)

---

#### 0:00-2:00 — Opening (Build Credibility Fast)

> "Thanks for taking the call. I want to be upfront about why I reached out.
> 
> I specialize in building automated lead-to-onboarding systems for B2B trading companies in Kenya. I came across Sokogate while researching the wholesale import space, and I spent some time going through your customer journey — your website, your social content, your app listing on Google Play.
> 
> I noticed a few things that I think could be optimized to grow your pipeline. I'm not here to sell you anything — I want to share what I found and see if it resonates."

**Why this works:** You immediately establish yourself as someone who has done the work. You're not reading from a script about generic services. You're talking about *their* specific business.

---

#### 2:00-10:00 — The "Mirror" (Show Them Their Own Gaps)

> "Let me share my screen and walk through what I observed."

**Show them their website:**
> "Here's ultimotradingltd.co.ke. A prospective wholesaler in Mombasa or Kampala lands here. They're interested in sourcing products from China. They scroll through. They have questions — about pricing, minimum order quantities, shipping timelines, customs clearance.
> 
> Where do they go?
> 
> Right now, they have one option: email bangali@ultimotradingltd.co.ke. In my experience, that means:
> - Most prospects never email (too much friction)
> - Those who do email wait hours or days for a reply
> - There's no qualification — you don't know if they're a serious buyer until you've already spent time on them
> - There's no follow-up — if they don't reply to your first email, that lead is dead"

**Show them their social media:**
> "Then look at your Instagram. You're posting 3-4 times a week. Educational content. Product showcases. People are commenting, asking questions. But I don't see any automated response or DM follow-up.
> 
> Every comment that goes unanswered is a potential bulk buyer who gave up and emailed a competitor instead."

**Show them the Sokogate app:**
> "The app itself is solid — but onboarding a new wholesaler to the app is a manual process. They download it, they register, but there's no guided onboarding flow. No automated check-in at day 1, day 3, day 7 to make sure they place their first order."

**The key insight to land:**
> "So here's what I'm seeing: **you're generating interest, but you're not capturing it.** Your marketing spend and content creation are feeding a funnel that leaks at every stage — from first visit to first order."

---

#### 10:00-20:00 — Discovery Questions (Diagnose Deeper)

Transition from your observations to their internal view:

> "That's what I see from the outside. I'd love to understand what's happening on the inside. Can I ask you a few questions?"

**Question 1: Volume & Sources**
> "In a typical month, how many new leads or inquiries do you get — roughly? And where do they come from? Website, app downloads, social media DMs, referrals?"

**Question 2: Current Process**
> "Can you walk me through what happens when a new lead comes in? Step by step — who handles it, what do they do, how long does it take?"

**Question 3: Follow-Up**
> "What happens to leads that don't convert right away? Do you have a system to follow up with them — weekly, monthly?"

**Question 4: Biggest Pain Point**
> "If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about how leads move through your pipeline, what would it be?"

**Question 5: Current Tools**
> "What tools are you using today — CRM, email marketing, WhatsApp Business, anything?"

**Question 6: Team & Capacity**
> "How many people handle sales and onboarding right now? Are they overwhelmed? Are there things they're doing manually that feel repetitive?"

**Question 7: Cost of Leakage**
> "If a qualified wholesaler — someone ready to place a 500K KES order — came in and then disappeared because the follow-up was slow, what would that cost you in a month? In a quarter?"

---

#### 20:00-25:00 — The Opportunity Quantification

Based on their answers, do quick math with them. Example:

> "So if you're getting roughly X inquiries per month, and based on what I see on the website/social there's probably [X] you're not even counting, and your current response time is [X hours/days]...
> 
> Industry data shows that responding to a lead within 5 minutes vs. 24 hours increases conversion by 9x.
> 
> If we assume a conservative improvement of even 3x on your current conversion rate, and your average wholesale order is [their typical ticket size]...
> 
> That's [X] KES in additional revenue per month that's sitting on the table because the pipeline doesn't have the right automation."

**Then transition to the offer:**

> "Here's what I'd like to propose: Let me do a full Pipeline Audit. I'll spend 2-3 hours mapping your entire lead flow — every touchpoint, every handoff, every gap. I'll quantify the leakage in revenue terms. And I'll give you a roadmap of exactly what automation would fix it.
> 
> No cost. No obligation. If the data shows it's worth it, we talk about next steps. If not, you have a free audit you can use however you want.
> 
> Does that sound fair?"

---

#### 25:00-30:00 — Close & Commitment

> "Great. To do the audit properly, I'll need access to a few things:
> 
> 1. Any lead records or CRM data you have (even if it's just a spreadsheet)
> 2. Access to the Sokogate app backend or any analytics you have
> 3. A 15-minute follow-up with whoever handles day-to-day sales
> 4. Permission to go through your social media DMs and comment history
> 
> When can I get those? And when should I schedule the follow-up to present the audit findings?"

**Book the follow-up immediately using QMe:**
> "Let me send you a link to book the follow-up call — pick whatever time works for you."

## PART 4: The Data You Need to Collect (The Audit Checklist)

After the call, here's every piece of data you need to gather before you can build anything.

### Section A: Lead Volume & Sources

| Data Point | Why You Need It |
|---|---|
| Monthly lead volume (all sources) | Baseline for calculating improvement |
| Breakdown by source (website, app, social, referral, walk-in, phone) | Tells you which channels to prioritize |
| Monthly website visitors | Conversion rate calculation |
| Monthly app downloads | Drop-off from download to first order |
| Social media follower count + engagement rate | Capture rate from social to pipeline |
| Number of incoming WhatsApp messages per month | Current WhatsApp usage (even if manual) |

### Section B: Lead Handling Process

| Data Point | Why You Need It |
|---|---|
| Current lead response time (minutes/hours/days) | Single biggest leverage point for automation |
| Lead assignment: Who gets which leads? Is it random? | Routing optimization opportunity |
| Lead qualification criteria: What makes a lead "good"? | Design the chatbot qualification questions |
| Number of touchpoints before a lead converts or dies | Design the follow-up sequence length |
| Current follow-up cadence and content | You'll improve on this |
| Lead status definitions used (if any) | Will map into your CRM stages |
| Tools currently used (CRM, spreadsheet, paper, memory?) | Integration requirements |

### Section C: Conversion Metrics

| Data Point | Why You Need It |
|---|---|
| Lead-to-opportunity conversion rate | Baseline KPI — you'll report improvement |
| Opportunity-to-onboarding conversion rate | Onboarding funnel efficiency |
| Onboarding-to-first-order conversion rate | Post-onboarding drop-off |
| Average sales cycle length (first touch to first order) | Speed improvement target |
| Average order value (first order & repeat) | Revenue quantification |
| Customer lifetime value (CLV) | Justifies retainer pricing |
| Lead-to-customer cost (acquisition cost) | Shows ROI of automation |

### Section D: Follow-Up & Nurture

| Data Point | Why You Need It |
|---|---|
| Do they follow up with unconverted leads? How? | If no, huge gap. If yes, likely manual and inconsistent |
| How long do they nurture before giving up? | Design your nurture sequence duration |
| Current re-engagement triggers (if any) | Can automate these |
| Do they have any email list or newsletter? | Email nurturing opportunity |
| WhatsApp broadcast list usage? | WhatsApp nurturing opportunity |

### Section E: Onboarding Flow

| Data Point | Why You Need It |
|---|---|
| Steps a new client goes through to start using Sokogate | Map into QMe onboarding sequence |
| Time from signup to first order | Baseline for automation improvement |
| Documents/info needed from new clients | Create automated document collection flow |
| Who handles onboarding? One person? Manual? | Capacity bottleneck |
| Drop-off points in onboarding | Where to focus automation effort |
| Common questions/complaints during onboarding | Pre-answer in automated flow |

### Section F: Technical & Access

| Data Point | Why You Need It |
|---|---|
| Do they have access to their website backend? | Embedding chatbot |
| Who manages their social media accounts? | Automating social DM responses |
| Do they have a WhatsApp Business account? | API setup requirements |
| What's their domain for email? Setting up proper sending? | Email automation prerequisites |
| Do they use any accounting/inventory software? | Potential integration points |
| Do they have Google Analytics installed? | Traffic data source |

**Your line if they hesitate:**

> "I understand. Here's what I'll do — I'll build a small demo of what the automated WhatsApp capture would look like for Sokogate. It'll take me a couple of hours. I'll send you a video showing it in action. If you like it, we talk. If not, no hard feelings."

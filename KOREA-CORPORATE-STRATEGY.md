# Korea Corporate Procurement Strategy: Supply Chain Diversification Through Sokogate

## Executive Summary

**The Opportunity:** Korean mid-sized manufacturers and chaebol procurement teams are actively seeking to diversify their supply chains away from China dependency. African commodities — agricultural products, minerals, and manufactured goods — represent an untapped sourcing corridor. Sokogate's B2B platform bridges African exporters with Korean buyers. Mapato powers the automation layer that makes this corridor efficient at scale.

**The Strategy:**
1. **Target 10 Korean corporate customers** (procurement teams at mid-sized manufacturers & chaebols)
2. **Pilot with 20 African exporters/importers** on 3-month free Sokogate Platform trials
3. **Mapato automates** the qualification, compliance, and onboarding for both sides

**Revenue Target from Korean Corridor: $15,000–$25,000/mo within 12 months**

## 8. Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|--------|------------|
| Korean companies slow to adopt African suppliers | Slow pipeline | Start with Korean trading houses (already import from Africa), then expand to manufacturers |
| African exporters lack Korea-required certifications | Low compliance readiness | Pre-screen ERS scores. Offer certification facilitation as a service. Focus on exporters with at least 1 cert |
| Sokogate platform not ready for Korea corridor | No platform to pilot on | Work with Sokogate to prioritize Korea corridor features. Mapato fills gaps with compliance + automation layer |
| Language/cultural barriers Korea↔Africa | Communication friction | Korean-language materials for procurement teams. Mapato docs in English + Korean. Use Korean agents/partners for outreach |
| Pilot conversion lower than 50% | Revenue shortfall | Over-recruit pilots (target 25, expect 20). Shorten trial to 2 months for fast movers. Offer incentives for early conversion |
